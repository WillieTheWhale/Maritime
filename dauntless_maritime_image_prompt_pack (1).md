## 1. Global Art Direction

Create images that feel like a refined Outer Banks soundside expedition brand, not generic tropical tourism. The visual world should be quiet, cinematic, and place-specific: shallow protected water, marsh grass, weathered docks, crab-pot markers, navigation posts, soundside boardwalks, soft wind texture on the water, low landforms, and broad sky.

### Required style

- Photorealistic editorial tourism photography.
- Calm, premium, understated, and natural.
- Filmic but clean, not gritty.
- Wide negative space for typography.
- Accurate shallow-water coastal North Carolina environment.
- No exaggerated yachts, party scenes, tropical palms, mountains, cliffs, neon sunsets, Caribbean water, or crowded beaches.

### Color palette

- Deep sound navy: `#082F3A`
- Oyster white: `#F6F1E7`
- Marsh gold: `#C6964A`
- Weathered red: `#8B2E2B`
- Seafoam gray-green: `#A8C7BE`
- Deep pine: `#133B36`

### Lighting

- Sunrise: low amber light, pale blue sky, thin mist over marsh.
- Midday: clear but soft, coastal haze, no harsh tropical saturation.
- Sunset: warm gold, peach, muted rose, reflective sound water.
- Overcast: elegant gray-blue, light breeze, professional not gloomy.

### Camera rules

- Use realistic camera settings.
- Prefer 24-70 mm and 35 mm editorial realism.
- Use drone angles sparingly and only where specified.
- Keep horizon straight.
- Avoid fisheye distortion.
- For hero images, leave clear negative space on the left or right for text.

### Wildlife safety rules

- Wild horses must be at least 50 feet from humans and boat.
- No feeding, touching, petting, crowding, chasing, or posing beside horses.
- If horses appear, depict them naturally grazing, walking, or standing near dunes from a respectful distance.
- Do not show the boat beaching near horses.
- Do not imply guaranteed close wildlife encounters.
